# Decoding Motor Imagery from EEG Signals: An Evaluation of CSP-LDA vs. SVM Architectures

**Independent Research Project**  
**Intersection of Computational Neuroscience & Machine Learning**

## Abstract
This repository contains the methodology, data processing pipelines, and machine learning models for an independent study in Brain-Computer Interfaces (BCI). The project evaluates the viability of decoding motor imagery (imagining left vs. right fist movement) from raw electroencephalography (EEG) data. 

The study contrasts two machine learning architectures: a generalized cross-subject model utilizing a Support Vector Machine (SVM), and an optimized intra-subject model utilizing Common Spatial Patterns (CSP) paired with Linear Discriminant Analysis (LDA). The results demonstrate the necessity of subject-specific calibration in neurotechnology, highlighting the phenomenon of "BCI Illiteracy" in human-computer interaction.

## Dataset
The data is sourced from the **PhysioNet EEG Motor Movement/Imagery Dataset**, accessed via the `mne-python` library. 
* **Subjects:** 20 human subjects.
* **Task:** Subjects were instructed to imagine opening and closing either their left or right fist based on visual cues.
* **Recording:** 64-channel EEG recorded continuously and stored in EDF format.

## Methodology & Pipeline Optimization

### 1. The Baseline Model (Cross-Subject SVM)
Initially, a generalized model was built to decode brainwaves across all subjects simultaneously. 
* **Preprocessing:** Downsampled to 80Hz, filtered (7-30 Hz).
* **Architecture:** Common Spatial Patterns (CSP) for feature extraction + Linear Support Vector Machine (SVM).
* **Result:** **~49.94% Accuracy**. The model failed to generalize, performing no better than random chance due to the high variance in individual human skull thickness, cortical folding, and neural patterns.

### 2. The Optimized Model (Intra-Subject CSP-LDA)
To solve the generalization failure, the architecture was pivoted to an intra-subject, calibrated approach.
* **Band-Pass Filtering:** Narrowed to **8-15 Hz** to strictly isolate the Mu-rhythm (sensorimotor rhythm) associated with motor cortex activity.
* **Epoch Truncation:** Narrowed the time window to **0.0 - 2.0 seconds** post-cue to eliminate noise generated by cognitive fatigue.
* **Architecture:** Swapped the SVM for **Linear Discriminant Analysis (LDA)**, which performs better on small, high-variance biological datasets.

## Results
The optimized CSP-LDA intra-subject pipeline achieved a significantly higher average accuracy of **~59.8% to 60.8%**. 

More importantly, the intra-subject variance proves the neuro-cognitive phenomenon of **BCI Illiteracy**:
* **Highly Decodable Subjects:** Subjects 9, 14, and 15 achieved accuracies between **71.1% and 75.5%**, indicating they produce highly readable Mu-rhythms when imagining movement.
* **BCI Illiterate Subjects:** Other subjects scored near 44%, indicating their sensorimotor rhythms are too weak or noisy for standard non-invasive EEG classification.

*(A visualization of these results is available in the `/results` directory).*
